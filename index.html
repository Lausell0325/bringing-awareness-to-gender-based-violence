What is Gender-Based Violence?

Gender-based violence occurs when a person demonstrates behaviors that cause physical, sexual, or psychological harm to another person. It is motivated by gender stereotypes created by men and women. The concept of gender violence can include threats, aggression, persecution and isolation, among other similar actions. In most cases, the victims are women in situations of violence perpetrated by men.


Most common types of Gender- Based Violence

Gender-Based Violence is a very sensitive topic that is seen as very common and might be from people that you might not expect but how can we know if it is gender based violence , here are some types of violence:


  1. Physical violence: "This type of violence is an act attemting to cause or result in pain or injury".

  2. Verbal violence: "The act of an attacker using words to put down or harrass a victim (this type of violence also includes hate speech)""

  3. Pyschological violence: "All types of violence have a phycological aspect for violence but pychological violence can include: isolation, confinement , withholding information , disinformation or threatening behavior".

  4. Sexual Violence: "engaging in nonconsensual acts of sexual nature or forcing somebody elso to engage in them with a third party".

  5. Socio-economic violence:“where any form of unwanted verbal, nonverbal or physical conduct of a sexual nature occurs, with the purpose or effect of violating the dignity of a person, in particular when creating an intimidating, hostile, degrading, humiliating or offensive environment”.
